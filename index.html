The Man.

The Legend.

Born on November 17, 1972 in Split, Yugoslavia (now Croatia), with Italian blood flowing through his veins (his grandfather was Italian — which explains everything), Đovani Roso arrived in Israel and did what Italians always do: he made everyone fall in love with him.

Standing 1.87m tall, speaking fluent Hebrew, and playing attacking midfield with the grace of a man who knows he's being watched — Đovani wasn't just a footballer. He was a phenomenon.

"Of 7,000 fans at a Haifa game, approximately 6,000 were women."

October 29, 2002 — A Night That Changed Everything

The Night We
Broke
Manchester

3 — 0
🟢 Maccabi Haifa Man United 🔴
UEFA Champions League Group Stage · October 29, 2002

The first Israeli club ever to reach the UEFA Champions League group stage. They were drawn against Manchester United, Bayer Leverkusen, and Olympiacos. The world expected them to be polite guests. Instead, Maccabi Haifa beat Manchester United three goals to nil at home.

Đovani, wearing #11, was right in the middle of it all. Because of course he was. He scored 4 goals and 4 assists across 14 Champions League appearances that season. David Beckham's jersey mysteriously disappeared after that match. Nobody can explain this. Đovani smiles when asked about it.
